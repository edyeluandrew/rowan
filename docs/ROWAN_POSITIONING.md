### *Borderless Value. Local Payouts.*

## 0. The one-line position

**Rowan is the safe rails from digital dollars to local money — protected every step.**

We position by the **problem we own** (trusted conversion), not by the **map we cover** (geography). Geography is where we *start*, not who we *are*.

---

## 1. Core positioning principle

Most on/off-ramp startups position by geography ("an East Africa crypto app"). That is weak — a boundary is not a value proposition. Rowan positions by the job:

> Turn digital dollars into money you can actually spend — without getting scammed.

Blockchain is the plumbing; it stays invisible. To the user, Rowan is a clean fintech wallet: **digital dollars → mobile money**, escrow-protected.

## 3. Who we are for (the wedge)

**Primary wedge:** remote workers, freelancers and online earners who receive **USDC** and need **mobile money this week** for rent, food, bills.

Why them:
- Acute, recurring pain (weekly/monthly cash-out).
- Currently stuck between sketchy WhatsApp OTC desks (scam risk) and global exchanges (crypto UX, freeze risk).
- Tight, referral-driven communities — trust spreads fast.

**Secondary (later):** diaspora remittance recipients; crypto-native earners; then **B2B/API** (payroll, NGOs, online businesses paying African contractors).

## 5. Geography: start narrow, architect wide

**We do not position as "East Africa forever" or "global from day one."**
We start in **one corridor**, and design so each new corridor is a repeatable playbook — not a rebuild.

### 5.1 Why narrow wins
- A marketplace dies without **liquidity density**: 100 users + 5 great partners in one corridor beats 1,000 users scattered across six countries with nobody to match them.
- Trust is **local and word-of-mouth**.
- Regulation is **per-country** — "East Africa" is three legal regimes, not one market.

### 5.2 Expansion rings

| Ring | Scope | Unlock condition |
|---|---|---|
| **Ring 0 (now)** | Kenya · M-Pesa · USDC cash-out · freelancers | Legal opinion + pilot readiness |
| **Ring 1** | Kenya full (+ Airtel); begin Tanzania sandbox | Kenya traction proven |
| **Ring 2** | Uganda (when late-2026 framework lands); Rwanda | Uganda framework / sandbox live |
| **Ring 3** | West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana) — deep USDC demand | Rails + compliance repeatable |
| **Ring 4** | Other mobile-money economies | B2B API productized |

### 5.3 Market backdrop
Africa receives ~$100B/yr in remittances at ~7–9% fees, with mobile money as the dominant rail. Stablecoin rails cut that cost materially. This is the long-term prize — captured **one corridor at a time**, not all at once.

## 6. What expansion actually comes with

Every new country is effectively a new company. Each corridor requires:

1. **New regulator / license** — separate legal opinion, possibly local entity + capital requirements.
2. **New AML / sanctions / Travel-Rule posture** per jurisdiction.
3. **New liquidity cold-start** — partners don't transfer across borders; re-seed supply each time.
4. **New payment rails** — different MoMo APIs, fees, quirks, settlement.
5. **New trust-building from zero** — reputation is local.
6. **New FX corridor + local-currency settlement rules** (e.g., Tanzania GN 198 mandates TZS settlement).
7. **New ops load** — dispute coverage in local hours.

**Implication:** expanding as a *retail app per country* is slow and expensive. Expanding as **rails / API** that partners and businesses plug into is how it scales. The retail app **learns** the corridor; the **API monetizes across** corridors. (This is why Yellow Card pivoted retail → B2B.)

## 7. How we win the market

1. **Own "safe" — not "cheap," not "crypto."** Compete on protection, completion %, and payout speed. In a market full of OTC scams and after Paxful's collapse, "safe" is the open lane.
2. **Be the compliant one — loudly.** Kenya legalized this lane; informal desks can't follow. Make "operating within the VASP framework" part of the pitch, not fine print.
3. **Look like money, not crypto.** Clean fintech UX; blockchain invisible; plain language.

---

## 8. Moats (guard these — none are code)
- **Trust reputation** (dispute outcomes, completion rate).
- **Liquidity density** (great partners, fast fills, in-corridor).
- **Compliance posture** (licensed lane competitors can't enter).

---

## 9. Bottom line
The company that wins is **not** the one in the most countries. It is the one that **one specific group of people trusts completely** — then quietly becomes the rails everyone else builds on.

- Position: **safe rails from digital dollars to local money.**
- Start: **Kenya · M-Pesa · freelancers** — density over breadth.
- Architect: every corridor a repeatable playbook.
- Scale: via the **B2B payout API**.
- Guard: trust, liquidity, compliance.
